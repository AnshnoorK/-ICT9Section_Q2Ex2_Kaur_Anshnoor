Jisoo, Jennie, Ros√©, and Lisa are members of the South Korean girl group YG Entertainment. They are known as the "biggest girl group in the world" and are widely regarded as the most successful Korean girl group in the world, as well as one of the leaders of the Korean Wave. They are stylistically associated with the "girl crush" concept in K-pop, with themes of self-confidence and female empowerment explored.



Their works

Blackpink made their debut in August 2016 with their single album Square One, which included the number-one singles "Whistle" and "Boombayah" in South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart and the US Billboard World Digital Songs chart, respectively. In November, the single album Square Two was released, with the song "Playing with Fire" becoming the first by a Korean girl group to enter the Billboard Canadian Hot 100. With "Ddu-Du Ddu-Du" (2018), the group expanded their global reach, becoming the first Korean female group to have a song enter the UK Singles Chart and receive a certification in the United States. Its music video was the first by a Korean group to reach one and two billion views on YouTube, and it continues to be the most-viewed by a Korean group.
